How to ascertain the legal status of the applicant while applying for a trademark?

A & B law Venture

Introduction

The applicant's legal status determines the appropriate category under which the application under form TM-A is filed and impacts the filing fees. Where the applicant is an Individual / Startup/Small Enterprise concessions or reduced fees compared to other business entities such as large companies or foreign applicants.

♣♣♣♣In case of Startup♣♣♣♣

According to the Department of Promotion of Industry and Internal Trade (DPIIT)A startup is an entity.

  1. That is incorporated as a private limited company, a partnership firm, or a limited liability partnership (LLP). The entity has been operating for less than ten years from its incorporation or registration date. Whose turnover for any financial year since its incorporation has not exceeded INR 100 crore. That is working towards innovation, deployment, development, or commercialization of new processes, products, or services driven by technology or intellectual property

♣♣♣♣In case of Small Enterprise:♣♣♣♣

  1. Businesses that fall under the MSME classification based on investment and turnover thresholds under Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises Development Act, 2006 (MSMED Act).

♣♣♣♣In case of Individual Business:♣♣♣♣

Individual business can be of two types Sole proprietor and One person Company in India

  1. Solo Propteiership: It is the simplest business carried on by an individual. The individual establishing a sole proprietorship business is personally liable for its debts. A sole proprietorship does not have a legal entity like an LLC, OPC or a company. No compulsory registration, does not require a nominee, no directors required for such businesses and Taxed in the individual slab rate
  2. One person Company: One Person Company (OPC) under The Companies Act, 2013 is a hybrid of a sole proprietorship business and a company. It Should be registered under the Companies Act, 2013 on the MCA website, has a separate legal status, requires a minimum of one nominee to establish an OPC, minimum of one director is required and Filings with the Registrar of Companies (ROC) as per the Companies Act, 2013 and Income Tax Act
    When does a firm come under the category of MSME?

    In India, a firm is categorized as a Micro, Small, or Medium Enterprise (MSME) under the Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises Development Act, 2006 (MSMED Act) based on the investment in plant and machinery or equipment and annual turnover, as per the revised classification introduced in the MSME Notification 2020

    1. Micro Enterprise: A micro enterprise is defined as a business with limited investments in plant and machinery or equipment, operating in manufacturing, services, or trading sectors.
    2. Small Enterprise: A small enterprise is characterized by a higher investment capacity and turnover threshold than micro enterprises, allowing it to engage in manufacturing, services, or trading
    3. Medium Enterprise: A medium enterprise has the highest investment and turnover limits within the MSME classification, supporting operations in manufacturing, services, or trading
    Type of Enterprise Investment MSME Turnover Limit
    Micro Enterprise Rs 1 crore Rs 5 crore
    Small Enterprise Rs 10 crore Rs 50 crore
    Medium Enterprise Rs 50 crore Rs 250 crore
    ♣♣♣♣Conclusions♣♣♣♣

      Investment and turnover criteria: Both conditions (investment and turnover) must be satisfied to classify as an MSME. However, if a business exceeds these limits, it will no longer qualify for that category

      Businesses that do not fall under MSME?

      According to the MSME Ministry, organisations that do not directly deal with goods manufacturing or service delivery do not fall under the small business categories list. These include betting and gambling activities, logging and forestry, fishing and aquaculture, motorcycle repair or trade, household activities for domestic personnel, extraterritorial organisations, and goods and services that households produce for personal use.
